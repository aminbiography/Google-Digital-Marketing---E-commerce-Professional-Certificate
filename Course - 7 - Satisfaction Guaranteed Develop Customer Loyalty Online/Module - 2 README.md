# **Analyzing Trends for an E-Commerce Store**

---

### **E-Commerce Analytics Overview**

* Analytics help evaluate store performance, customer behavior, and sales trends.
* Data-driven decisions improve revenue, customer experience, and marketing effectiveness.

---

### **Shopify Analytics**

* **Dashboard:** Shows sales, orders, and visitors.
* **Reports:** Allow data export, print, and sharing for sales, customers, and inventory.
* **Live View:** Monitors real-time visitor activity and sales.
* **Google Analytics Integration:** Provides deeper insights into customer behavior.

---

### **Using Data to Improve Store Performance**

* Focus on **qualified traffic** (visitors likely to buy).
* Use **SEO** and **email marketing** to attract the right customers.
* **Conversion rate optimization (CRO)** improves how many visitors buy.
* **A/B testing** compares versions of pages to find what performs best.
* **Cross-selling** and **upselling** help increase **average order value (AOV)**.

---

### **Conversion Rate Optimization**

* CRO increases the % of visitors completing desired actions.
* **Micro conversions:** small actions (add to cart, watch video).
* **Macro conversions:** final purchase.
* Tools include:

  * **Heat maps:** show click and scroll behavior.
  * **Session recordings:** track real user interactions.
  * **A/B tests:** test webpage variations.
* Improve CRO by simplifying design, clear CTAs, fast loading, and mobile optimization.

---

### **Case Study – Kapa Nui Nails**

* Small eco-friendly brand using **ROI** and **CPC** to measure ad performance.
* Found **shopping ads** delivered the highest ROI (3:1) and CPC under $1.
* Used data tools like **Google Analytics** and **Google Tag Manager** to make informed marketing decisions.

---

### **Understanding Visitor Behavior with Heat Maps**

* **Scroll maps:** show how far visitors scroll.
* **Click maps:** show where visitors click or tap.
* **Hover maps:** show where visitors focus attention.
* Used together, they help improve page layout, CTAs, and user experience.

---

### **Product Analytics**

* Monitors product-specific performance and customer interaction.
* Key metrics:

  * **Views**, **add-to-carts**, **units purchased**, **conversion rate**, **revenue**.
  * **Net profit margin**, **ROAS**, **AOV**, and **return rate**.
* Helps improve product listings, pricing, and inventory decisions.

---

### **Optimizing Product Offerings**

* Use data to **increase product views** and **conversion rates**.
* Improve product pages with:

  * High-quality images, detailed descriptions, videos, and reviews.
* Discover new product ideas through:

  * Keyword searches and customer feedback.
* Retire underperforming SKUs to focus on high-demand products.

---

### **Glossary Highlights**

* **A/B testing:** Compare two versions to see which performs better.
* **ROI / ROAS:** Measure advertising efficiency.
* **CPC / CPA / CAC:** Track cost-related marketing metrics.
* **LTV / Retention rate:** Measure customer loyalty and long-term value.
* **Heat map / Session recording:** Visual tools to understand user behavior.
* **KPI:** Metrics used to track success toward goals.

---

### **Mathematical & Analytical Terms in E-Commerce**

#### **1. Conversion and Performance Metrics**

* **Conversion Rate (CR):**
  [
  \text{(Conversions ÷ Total Visitors)} × 100
  ]
  Measures how many visitors complete a desired action (like a purchase).

* **Cart Abandonment Rate (CAR):**
  [
  \text{(Carts Abandoned ÷ Carts Created)} × 100
  ]
  Indicates the percentage of users who add items but don’t complete checkout.

* **Customer Retention Rate (CRR):**
  [
  \frac{(E - N)}{S} × 100
  ]
  *E = Customers at end of period, N = New customers, S = Customers at start.*

* **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV or LTV):**
  [
  \text{Average Purchase Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan}
  ]
  Measures long-term revenue potential from one customer.

---

#### **2. Cost & Return Metrics**

* **Cost Per Acquisition (CPA):**
  [
  \text{Total Cost of Conversions ÷ Number of Conversions}
  ]

* **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**
  [
  \text{Total Sales & Marketing Cost ÷ Number of Customers Acquired}
  ]

* **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS):**
  [
  \text{Revenue from Ads ÷ Ad Spend}
  ]
  Example: ROAS of 4:1 means $4 earned for every $1 spent.

* **Return on Investment (ROI):**
  [
  \frac{(\text{Revenue} - \text{Cost})}{\text{Cost}} × 100
  ]
  Shows the profitability of marketing or business efforts.

---

#### **3. Revenue & Order Metrics**

* **Average Order Value (AOV):**
  [
  \text{Total Revenue ÷ Number of Orders}
  ]
  Indicates how much customers spend per transaction.

* **Net Profit Margin:**
  [
  \frac{\text{Net Profit}}{\text{Total Revenue}} × 100
  ]
  Shows how much profit remains after expenses.

* **Product Conversion Rate:**
  [
  \frac{\text{Product Purchases}}{\text{Product Page Views}} × 100
  ]
  Measures how effectively a product page converts visitors.

* **Return Rate:**
  [
  \frac{\text{Units Returned}}{\text{Units Sold}} × 100
  ]
  Indicates the percentage of sold products that were returned.

---

#### **4. Growth and Trend Analysis**

* **Quarter-over-Quarter (QoQ) Growth:**
  [
  \frac{(\text{Current Quarter Metric} - \text{Previous Quarter Metric})}{\text{Previous Quarter Metric}} × 100
  ]
  Measures performance change between two time periods.

* **Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth:**
  [
  \frac{(\text{Current Year Metric} - \text{Previous Year Metric})}{\text{Previous Year Metric}} × 100
  ]
  Tracks annual growth or decline.

---

#### **5. Engagement & Email Metrics**

* **Open Rate:**
  [
  \text{(Emails Opened ÷ Emails Sent)} × 100
  ]
* **Click-to-Open Rate (CTOR):**
  [
  \text{(Clicks ÷ Opens)} × 100
  ]
* **Unsubscribe Rate:**
  [
  \text{(Unsubscribes ÷ Emails Delivered)} × 100
  ]
* **Complaint Rate:**
  [
  \text{(Complaints ÷ Emails Delivered)} × 100
  ]
* **Email Bounce Rate:**
  [
  \text{(Undelivered Emails ÷ Emails Sent)} × 100
  ]

---

#### **6. Advertising & Traffic Metrics**

* **Cost Per Click (CPC):**
  [
  \text{Total Ad Spend ÷ Number of Clicks}
  ]
* **Click-Through Rate (CTR):**
  [
  \text{(Clicks ÷ Impressions)} × 100
  ]
* **Qualified Traffic Rate:**
  [
  \text{(Qualified Visitors ÷ Total Visitors)} × 100
  ]
  Measures how much of your traffic is likely to convert.
