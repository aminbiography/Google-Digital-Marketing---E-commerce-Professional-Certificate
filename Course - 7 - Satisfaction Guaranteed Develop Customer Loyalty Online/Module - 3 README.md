### **Rise of E-commerce (2020–2021)**

Global lockdowns in 2020 caused a surge in online shopping, with digital buyers reaching **2.14 billion** in 2021 — over one-quarter of the world’s population. This growth created many new e-commerce job opportunities. The course helps prepare for such roles through portfolio building, resume writing, and interview preparation.

A detailed marketing report was created for the **Sinclair Verde Lamp Company**, managed by Good Mercury Marketing. The project highlighted analytical, presentation, and reporting skills.

### **Case Study – ShearShare**

ShearShare initially used the wrong keyword (“rentable chair”) in its Google Ads, attracting irrelevant users. After changing to **“salon chairs for rent,”** the uninstall rate dropped from **70% to 25%**. Lesson: Analyze metrics and adjust ad strategies to target the right audience.

### **6. Creating a Portfolio**

Erika from Google explained that digital marketers and e-commerce professionals should have a **portfolio** showcasing their best work (ads, social posts, email campaigns, etc.). Portfolios can be **websites**, **slideshows**, or **printed documents**. Key tips: organize projects, summarize outcomes, show transferable skills, and demonstrate a willingness to learn.

---

### **Google Sites Portfolio**

**Google Sites** is a free, easy-to-use tool for creating digital portfolios. It offers customizable layouts, responsive pages, embedded content (like resumes or Google Drive files), and publishing options. You can share your portfolio via a unique link on your resume to showcase your work professionally.

### **Finding Your Path & Perfect Role**

Digital marketing and e-commerce overlap but differ in focus — **marketing** promotes, while **e-commerce** sells. You can choose to be a **generalist** (broad skills) or a **specialist** (focused expertise). Career paths include working in **agencies**, **in-house teams**, or as a **freelancer**. Understanding your preferences helps you choose the right career direction.

---

### **Adding Relevant Skills to Resume**

Even if you meet only **50%** of a job’s requirements, you can still be considered for interviews by emphasizing what you know and showing a **growth mindset**. Use strong action verbs to describe basic and intermediate skills. Tailor your resume to roles such as:

* **Email Marketing Coordinator:** Use tools like Mailchimp.
* **Digital Marketing Coordinator:** Work with Google Ads, Sheets, and Constant Contact.
* **Analyst / E-commerce Associate:** Highlight Google Analytics, Tableau, and Shopify skills.
